THEORETICAL INVESTIGATION OF THE RUDLESDEN-POPPER OXINITRIDES STRUCTURES Sr2(Ta,Nb)O4 − xNx VIA DENSITY FUNCTIONAL THEORY

  1. ABATRACT:

    2. The Ruddlesden-Popper phase (rp) phase is a perovskite arranged in dislocated layers, with an intergrowth of ABX3 perovskite layers and an AX layer:(AX)(ABX3). Partial replacement of oxygen by nitrogen (due to similarities in electronegativity, polarizability, ionic radio) leads to modifications of electrical, optical, catalytic and magnetic properties. Additionally, a possible substitution-induced ferroelectric behaviour is anticipated.

    This work presents the analysis of the structural, electronic and phononic properties of the compound Sr2(Ta,Nb)O4 − xNx for two anion concentrations x = 0.5 y x = 1.0. This was achieved by means of dft implemented in the Vienna ab-initio simulation package (vasp) compiled in the "Guane" cluster of the supercomputer of the Industrial University of Santander. The substitution was carried out through the sod code obtaining 11 configurations for x = 1.0, and two configurations for x = 0.5, all with different anionic orders. Subsequently, structural relaxation was carried out, finding two configurations of minimum energy with trans and cis anionic ordering, belonging to the space group Immm (S.G. 71) and Cmcm (S.G. 63), respectively. dft Calculations indicate that the cis anion order is energetically more favourable than the trans order. The electronic structure suggests an insulating material with a reduction in bandgap at point Γ allowing photocatalysis in the visible range for water splitting. The phononic structure shows imaginary modes that lead to atomic displacements and a drop in symmetry, achieving a phase transition to the most favourable chiral soft mode with space group C2221 (SG. 20).

  2. SEARCH FOR THE MOST STABLE RP OXYNITRIDES:

    3. This chapter presents a structural analysis of the oxynitrides rp Sr2(Ta,Nb)O4 − xNx and the search for the most stable anionic orderings. It begins with a brief description of perovskite oxides rp, then describes the partial anionic substitution x = 0.5 of O2− by N3−, and the partial substitution x = 1 of O2− by N3−. The anionic substitutions are carried out using the sod package of tools , which is a package of tools for computational modeling of periodic systems with site substitutional disorder. A disordered system is modeled as a copy of the cell, where different combinations of disorder are placed at the crystallographic sites. The cell different from the original cell is called a ’configuration’. The process of identifying the symmetry of each configuration is performed with the symmetry operations of the crystallographic group to which it corresponds. Subsequently, symmetrically equivalent configurations are identified, and only configurations with unique symmetry are taken into account. Once the unique configurations are identified, the sod code shows the unique configurations, and their degeneracy, that is, the equivalent configurations .

    The criterion of equivalence between two configurations is based on the concept of isometric transformations, which are geometric operations such as translations, rotations, and reflections, that maintain all distances and angles within the transformed object constant. In Figure configurations, it can be seen that equivalent configurations are the same configuration because a transformation can be used to verify that a configuration is equal to the untransformed configuration. On the other hand, inequivalent configurations do not have a transformation to obtain an equivalent configuration. The fact that two configurations have the same space group does not imply that they will have the same energy 1, since the elements occupying the crystallographic sites may change, meaning that the energy of the structure depends on the site occupancy and interactions. Concluding the chapter, an analysis of the structural energy of each configuration is presented. Additionally, with the help of the program poscar2cif, the crystallographic space group of each configuration was obtained.

    The focus of this work is the Ruddlesden-Popper (rp) phase, which has a general formula of An + 1BnX3n + 1(n=1), where A and B represent the cations, and X are the anions in the structure<sup>1</sup>. The cations occupying the crystallographic sites are strontium (Sr) for site A, niobium or tantalum (Nb,Ta) for site B, and oxygen (O) for site X.
